before 1900

The first mention of Dante in China occurs in A Brief History of Europe (1886) published by Joseph Edkins. Joseph Edkins (1823-1905) was a British Protestant missionary and sinologist who spent 57 years in China.

Click to see full text

Eugenio Zanoni Volpicelli (1856-1936) was the first to translate the Divine Comedy into Chinese. Unfortunately, Volpicelli’s works were lost after his death, and this translation has yet to be found.
See Qi Chen, “Dante in the Chinese Context: Translation, Study and Teaching,” Forum Italicum: A Journal of Italian Studies 55, no. 2 (August 2021): 655.

1900-1910

Liang Qichao invoked Dante in the drama New Legend of Rome (1902) in the widely read biweekly journal Xinmin Congbao (New Citizen). In New Legend of Rome, Dante appears as the opening character in the prologue.
Liang Qichao. “The New Legend of Rome”. In New Citizen. no. 10. Japan, Yokohama: Guangzhi Press. 1902.

In her Writings in Retirement (1910), Shan Shili (1858–1945) recounted her visit to the tomb of Boniface VIII, noting that Dante had placed the pope in the Inferno.
